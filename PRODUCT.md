# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individual job seekers working alone, likely at home, often under deadline pressure. They may feel anxious or uncertain about their application. The primary task on any screen is: "how do I get this job?" They need clear, actionable signal, not more noise.

## Product Purpose

JobSeeker is an AI-powered resume analyser. Users upload a resume and a job description; the tool returns a match score broken down by component, keyword gaps, suggested section rewrites, and prioritised action items. Success looks like a user leaving with a clearer, stronger application and confidence to submit it.

## Brand Personality

Warm, honest, encouraging. Like a trusted friend who happens to be a career coach: they tell you the truth, but they're rooting for you. Not clinical, not hype-y, not cheerleader-shallow.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream: Notion-style white + gray, purple accent, identical card grids everywhere.
- Flashy AI product: dark mode with neon gradients, glowing cards, hero-metric templates.
- HR software: conservative corporate blues, formal enterprise typography, cold layouts.
- Gamified job boards: LinkedIn / Indeed energy, progress bars everywhere, badge-heavy UIs.

## Design Principles

1. **Reduce anxiety, not just friction.** Visual choices should calm the user down, not excite or overwhelm. Space, warmth, and restraint do this. Density and decoration undo it.
2. **Results feel like help, not a report.** The output of the analysis should read like advice from a person, not data from a dashboard. Typography and hierarchy reinforce this.
3. **Earn trust through precision.** Scores and labels must feel considered, not algorithmic. Clear rationale, honest language, no inflated encouragement.
4. **Input and output share equal weight.** The upload/input experience is as important as the results. If the entry feels clunky, users won't trust the output.
5. **One tool, one task.** No feature sprawl. Every UI element must trace back to helping the user improve this specific application.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigable throughout. Sufficient color contrast on all text and interactive elements. Screen reader compatible. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations.
