## Problem

The app will be functional after Phase 2, but built by an LLM in rapid execution mode — layout, spacing, typography, flow, and micro-interactions may be technically correct but not refined. A job seeker's first impression of the tool is the UX: if it feels clunky or unpolished, trust in the AI output suffers.

Needs a full UX pass: visual hierarchy, loading/error states, mobile layout, empty states, button placement, copy, panel sizing, streaming feedback quality, and accept/reject interaction design for rewrites.

## Solution

After Phase 2 (analysis panel live) and before Phase 3 (interview prep), do a structured UX review:

1. Run the app end-to-end with a real resume + JD
2. Document friction points and visual issues
3. Prioritise by impact (first impression > edge cases)
4. Fix in a focused pass — copy, spacing, component polish, loading states

Consider: does the streaming "thinking" text look like meaningful progress or noise? Are the rewrite diffs readable? Is the score card legible at a glance? Does the two-panel layout work on a laptop viewport?
